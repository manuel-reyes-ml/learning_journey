## Original Document (verbatim)

This is an exceptionally rigorous, production-focused roadmap. The "evidence-first, replace-not-stack" philosophy is exactly what the 2026 technical job market demands. Balancing an 8-to-5 schedule with intense upskilling is notoriously difficult, but your 25-hour-per-week timeboxing makes this 32-month trajectory highly sustainable.

Here is a complete review of your v10.0 roadmap, followed by a data-backed strategy for embedding yourself into the exact networks that generate referrals for Data Engineering and Applied AI/FDE roles.

### 📊 Comprehensive Roadmap Review (v10.0)

**The Strengths (What to Keep Unchanged)**

* **The FDE/Applied AI Framing:** Shifting away from a generic "ML Engineer" title to the Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) track is a highly strategic move. FDE roles heavily prioritize candidates who can bridge raw engineering with messy enterprise reality, which perfectly aligns with your ERISA-regulated finance operations background.
* **Architecture & Documentation Rigor:** Adding C4 models and Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) to your flagships is a massive differentiator. FDE interview loops are notorious for system design decomposition—turning "I built this" into "Here is why I chose X over Y, and here is how it handles late data" is exactly what hiring managers are looking for.
* **CS Foundations:** Relying on CS50 for foundational computer science and debugging rigor, combined with the OMSCS Computing Systems track, provides the necessary depth to pass the concurrency and distributed-system screens common in top-tier tech loops.
* **Domain-Specific Flagships:** Your *Crucible* project is a brilliant portfolio piece. The focus on the underlying structure of the market, rather than chasing algorithmic news sentiment, paired with a Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) kill switch, signals deep maturity to fintech hiring managers.
* **Explicit Fallback Triggers:** The strict Month 8 trigger (internal scope elevation or pivot to external search) prevents you from getting trapped in "tutorial purgatory" or waiting endlessly for a promotion that won't come.

**The Missing Link: Distribution**

The primary gap in your roadmap is **Distribution**. You are building a world-class, production-grade portfolio, but the code is only as good as the eyes on it. The roadmap solves for the *skills* required to pass the interview, but it lacks a structured mechanism to secure the *referral* that gets you the interview in the first place.

### 🤝 Strategic Networking & Referral Options

To transition effectively, your networking strategy must mirror your engineering strategy: high-signal, proof-based, and targeted. Here are the best, real-world avenues to grow your network based on current 2026 data.

#### 1. Official Cloud Provider Programs (AWS & Google)

These programs are not just for learning; they are active referral engines run by the cloud providers.

* **AWS Community Builders Program:**
  * **The Reality:** This is highly accessible for active builders. It is a curated collection of cloud enthusiasts that offers direct networking with AWS product teams.
  * **The Benefits:** Members receive $500 in AWS credits, a 50% discount on re:Invent tickets, one year of free CloudAcademy access, and a free AWS certification voucher.
  * **The Strategy:** Applications typically open annually in early January. Because acceptance is based on content creation and community sharing, you should document your *DataVault* AWS architectural decisions publicly (via a blog or LinkedIn) to build your application portfolio.
* **Google Developer Experts (GDE) - Data Cloud / AI:**
  * **The Reality:** GDE is an elite tier. To become a GDE, you *must* be referred by an existing GDE or a Google employee.
  * **The Strategy:** While GDE is a great long-term Stage 3 goal, your immediate step should be joining a local **Google Developer Group (GDG)**. Engaging with GDG organizers is the most reliable way to organically meet Googlers and secure the referral required for the GDE track.

#### 2. Local & Regional Dominance (The Carolinas)

Since you are based in Greenville but willing to relocate, you are positioned near major data engineering hubs (Charlotte and Atlanta), but you should start in your own backyard.

* **HackGreenville:**
  * **The Community:** This is the definitive developer network for Upstate SC, operating as a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit.
  * **The Mechanics:** They run an incredibly active Slack workspace with over 2,000 members. This is a zero-barrier entry point to find mentors, local peers, and unlisted tech jobs in the region.
  * **Actionable Step:** Join their Slack immediately and attend local, highly technical meetups like *Alt.Computing* or *UpstatePHP*. Local referrals often yield the highest interview conversion rates because the trust barrier is lower.
* **Charlotte & Atlanta Tech Hubs:** If your Month 10 fallback triggers an external search, Charlotte is a massive target for Analytics Engineering. 2026 market data shows high concentrations of open roles specifically demanding dbt, Snowflake, and Python in the Charlotte metro area.

#### 3. Deep-Tech & Practitioner Communities

FDE and Analytics Engineering roles are highly specialized; the best referrals come from the trenches.

* **The dbt Community Slack:**
  * **The Reality:** With roughly 50,000 members, this is the single largest concentration of practicing Analytics and Data Engineers on the internet.
  * **The Culture:** It is heavily curated and actively hostile to spam or shallow networking.
  * **The Strategy:** Once your *DataVault* project (Stage 2) is complete, post the C4 diagrams and your ADRs into the #i-made-this channel. Deep, technical transparency here routinely sparks direct messages from hiring managers.
* **Palantir & FDE Virtual Ecosystems:**
  * **The Reality:** The FDE title was pioneered by Palantir and requires a unique blend of heavy engineering and client-facing stakeholder management.
  * **The Strategy:** Palantir regularly hosts invite-only "AI Engineer Socials" (often in tech hubs like SF) and virtual early-talent recruiting events. Apply to attend these virtual events explicitly to network with their deployment specialists and hiring teams.
